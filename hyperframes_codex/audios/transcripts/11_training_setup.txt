For the reproducible setup, the base model was Flux2 Klein Base 4B, trained with ai-toolkit.

The training resolution was 768 by 768. The dataset used 70 paired samples. Batch size was 1, gradient accumulation was 1, the optimizer was AdamW 8-bit, the learning rate was 1e-4, and the LoRA rank was 32.

The run used 20,000 training steps on one RTX Pro 6000, taking about 6 hours.

One important finding was that more data did not automatically mean better results. A carefully selected set of around 50 pairs could already produce usable outputs, while thousands of randomly selected pairs made quality worse.

For this task, quality, diversity, and structural correctness were more important than raw dataset size.
